## Why This Structure Works

**1. The hero section earns attention in 3 seconds.**
Recruiters spend under 10 seconds on a profile. The capsule-render banner, single-sentence tagline, and badge row communicate identity instantly without making them scroll.

**2. Version 1 is scannable. Version 2 has voice.**
V1 uses tables, bullet points, and headers — optimized for recruiters who skim. V2 uses short paragraphs with conviction — optimized for founders, senior engineers, and startup teams who read.

**3. Projects lead with the problem, then the engineering.**
The "why it exists" framing (e.g., "LinkedIn is a feed. Bumble BFF is personal.") signals product thinking — something most student profiles completely skip.

**4. Numbers are specific, not inflated.**
"35% reduction" reads as real. "Dramatically improved performance" reads as resume padding. Specificity is credibility.

**5. The skills section uses icons, not a wall of text.**
skillicons.dev renders cleanly on any screen. Grouped by layer (languages, frontend, backend, AI), it reads at a glance without making the profile feel like a keyword list.

**6. The stats section is present but not the point.**
GitHub stats widgets are optional decorations — not proof of ability. Placed at the bottom, they add visual completeness without becoming the identity of the profile.

**7. The contact section is action-oriented.**
"I'm actively looking for roles in X, Y, Z" is more useful to a recruiter than "feel free to reach out." It states intent clearly and lists specific role types.

**Easiest way to build it:**
1. Open Figma → New frame → 1500 × 500
2. Use the gradient background, place text left-aligned with generous padding
3. Add subtle noise texture (Figma plugin: "Noise & Texture") at 3–5% opacity for depth
4. Export as PNG, upload to an image host (GitHub itself, or imgur), and reference in your README via `<img src="..."/>` at the top of the file
